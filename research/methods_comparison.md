\# 3D Reconstruction Methods



\## 1. COLMAP

\*\*Input:\*\* Multiple overlapping photos



\*\*Output:\*\* 3D point cloud and camera positions



\*\*Advantages:\*\*

\- High accuracy

\- Free and open source



\*\*Disadvantages:\*\*

\- Needs many good-quality images



\---



\## 2. NeRF (Neural Radiance Fields)



\*\*Input:\*\* Multiple images with camera positions



\*\*Output:\*\* Realistic 3D scene



\*\*Advantages:\*\*

\- Very realistic results



\*\*Disadvantages:\*\*

\- Requires a powerful GPU

\- Slower than some newer methods



\---



\## 3. Gaussian Splatting



\*\*Input:\*\* Images with camera information



\*\*Output:\*\* Fast 3D scene rendering



\*\*Advantages:\*\*

\- Fast rendering

\- High visual quality



\*\*Disadvantages:\*\*

\- Needs many images and good hardware



\---



\# Which method is most suitable?



I think COLMAP is a good starting point because it is beginner-friendly, open source, and widely used. Gaussian Splatting can later improve the visual quality and speed of rendering.
